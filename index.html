Itinerary

Aug 6 to Aug 10 family plan

Most days are designed so you can pivot to family visits around 4 PM. The Yosemite day is the only realistic all-day exception.
Aug 6 Arrival + light SF

Easy arrival day

  • Check in near SFO and reset
  • Late morning or early afternoon: Ferry Building and Embarcadero stroll
  • Optional quick stop: Palace of Fine Arts for photos
  • Be free by 4 PM for family
Aug 7 San Francisco must-see

Classic SF before family time

  • 8:30 AM Golden Gate Bridge vista from Battery East or Crissy Field
  • 10:00 AM Palace of Fine Arts and Presidio Tunnel Tops
  • 12:30 PM Fisherman’s Wharf or Ghirardelli lunch
  • Head back by 3 PM and be free by 4 PM
Aug 8 Napa day

Napa without losing the evening

  • 8:00 AM leave for Napa
  • 9:30 AM Oxbow Public Market breakfast and walk
  • 11:30 AM Yountville stroll or one scenic winery stop
  • Leave Napa by 2 PM and aim to be free around 4 PM
Aug 9 Full-day Yosemite

Yosemite day trip

  • 5:30 AM leave for Yosemite to beat traffic
  • Must-see stops: Tunnel View, Yosemite Valley, Valley View
  • Lower Yosemite Fall is iconic, but it is often dry by late July or August
  • Expect to return in the evening, not by 4 PM
Aug 10 Departure split day

Easy closeout near the airport

  • Slow breakfast in Burlingame or Millbrae
  • Optional short walk at Coyote Point or Bayfront area
  • 3 returning travelers head for flights
  • The remaining traveler can still use the late afternoon for family

Source notes

Planning reality checks

  • Yosemite says no reservation is required in 2026, but entry fees still apply
  • The park recommends arriving before 9 AM to reduce traffic
  • Lower Yosemite Fall is often dry from late July or August through October
